Industry Partners Grant Projects

Grant Opportunities for Universities and Industry Partners under the CHIPS Act
The CHIPS and Science Act, signed into law in August 2022, allocates billions of dollars to support the domestic semiconductor industry through various grants and incentives. Universities and industry partners can collaborate to tap into these funds by focusing on different program areas:


Semiconductor Manufacturing Incentives

  • CHIPS for America: Large Semiconductor Facility Investments
    • Program: Aims to incentivize construction, expansion, or modernization of commercial facilities for leading-edge, current-generation, and mature-node semiconductors.
    • Funding: Up to $50 billion in direct funding, loans, and loan guarantees.
    • Target Applicants: Semiconductor manufacturers and consortia (including university-industry partnerships).
    • Applications Closed: June 23, 2023 (new NOFO for smaller projects expected later).

Research and Development (R&D) Programs

  • National Semiconductor Technology Competition (NSTC)
    • Program: Funds collaborative research projects led by teams of researchers from universities and U.S. companies.
    • Funding: $11 billion through 2026.
    • Target Applicants: University-industry partnerships focused on critical areas like advanced logic, memory, and materials science.
    • Applications Open: Rolling basis (check latest NSTC website for details).
  • Semiconductor Industry and Technology Infrastructure Program (SITIP)
    • Program: Supports shared research infrastructure that benefits multiple entities, including universities.
    • Funding: $3 billion through 2026.
    • Target Applicants: Non-profit research institutions or consortia, including university-led groups.
    • Applications Open: Expected in early 2024.
  • NSF and Micron Foundation Partnership for the Robert Noyce Teacher Scholarship Program
    • Program: Supports scholarships for STEM teachers pursuing graduate studies in microelectronics or related fields.
    • Funding: $25 million over five years.
    • Target Applicants: Universities offering graduate programs in microelectronics or related fields.
    • Applications Open: Contact the National Science Foundation (NSF) for specifics.
  • Workforce Development Programs
    • $10 billion allocated for regional innovation and technology hubs, which could involve university involvement in training programs for semiconductor technicians and engineers.
    • National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) grants might support workforce development initiatives in collaboration with universities.

Key Tips for Success:

  • Demonstrate clear partnerships: Highlight the benefits of university-industry collaboration in your proposals.
  • Focus on critical areas: Align your projects with the CHIPS Act's key priorities like advanced technologies and workforce development.
  • Leverage existing expertise: Showcase your research strengths and industry connections to build a strong proposal.
  • Stay updated: Follow relevant websites and funding announcements for new opportunities.

Remember, grant competition is likely to be intense. By carefully tailoring our proposals to the program's objectives and showcasing the value of our university-industry partnership, we can increase our chances of securing CHIPS Act funding and contributing to the revitalization of the U.S. semiconductor industry.
